Shamir Secret Sharing

Split a secret into n shares; any k of them reconstruct it, fewer reveal nothing.

Offline · no server · your secret never leaves this tab

    Store the shares in different places. If you lose more than n−k of them the secret is unrecoverable.

    Reconstructed secret

    A (k, n) threshold scheme: the secret is the constant term of a secret polynomial of degree (k-1). Shares are points on that curve.

    • Any k shares determine the curve uniquely → the secret is recovered.
    • Fewer than k shares fit infinitely many curves → zero information (every secret equally likely).

    The secret is converted to UTF-8 bytes and each byte is shared independently in the GF(256) finite field (addition = XOR, multiplication modulo the AES polynomial). This prevents overflow and leakage.

    All computation runs inside your browser; nothing is sent anywhere. Randomness is cryptographic via crypto.getRandomValues. For educational use.
